Symbols of identity

Three threads of the Sindhu Spirit

Every part of who we are is woven from art, water, and devotion — rooted in one of the oldest living cultures on earth.

Textile · Identity

Ajrak — the sacred cloth

Block-printed in deep indigo and crimson with techniques passed down for millennia, Ajrak is the soul of Sindhi identity. Worn by farmers and presidents alike, its geometry encodes the stars, the rivers, and our beliefs. Every motif tells a story.

Water · Origin

The River Sindhu

The Indus gave Sindhis their name. Water is sacred — Cheti Chand begins at its edge, calling the river-god Jhulelal.

Devotion · Protection

Jhulelal

Seated on a lotus upon a Palla fish, Jhulelal — the water deity — is guardian of the Sindhi people. Every aarti calls his name.

Folklore · Courage

The Seven Queens of Sindh

In Shah Latif's Risalo, seven heroines stand for the strength of ordinary people — choosing love, loyalty, and dignity over fear. For centuries they have taught Sindhis, and especially Sindhi women, to stand tall.

Living culture

Rich in every sense

From the fragrance of Sindhi Kadhi to the dhamal at a Sufi shrine — our heritage is alive, joyful, and always shared.

Cuisine

Sindhi food

Daal Pakwan, Sindhi Kadhi, Koki, Sai Bhaji — flavours shaped by centuries of trade across the Middle East, Central Asia, and South Asia.

Music & poetry

Sufi music & Sindhi Bait

Sung poetry, the dhamal, and the soulful verses of Shah Latif — devotional, joyful, and deeply communal.

Craft

Ralli & handicrafts

Ralli patchwork quilts — stitched by women sharing stories — and intricate embroidery, passed mother to daughter.
